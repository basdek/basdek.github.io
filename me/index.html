Mastering hows

I am all about mastering hows. I offer two services: conception and transformation. Depending on the phase of your idea or company one of these takes precedence. However, I often do and can do both. What does that mean? And why would you want to work with me?

I have a background and experience as a software engineer, devops engineer, software architect and in leading teams of software engineers. I have the strong conviction that these experiences increase eachothers power. I was born with an entrepeneurial mindset and have employed that at every opportunity: At some point I discovered that whatever task I am given, or under whichever pretence I am hired, I will find where your organisation is hurting and will try to throw myself at that particular problem. Because I am aware, or will grow to be aware, that this pain point is hindering the tasks that you want completed in the first place.

If you feel that you have an idea that you want to become manifest, but are struggling to get your organisation in the shape to make it reality, you probably need my help. Pragmatic where I can and principled where I must I can guide your organisation towards the technical realisation of your idea. It is often said that execution is everything. After a why, after a what, comes the difficult how question. I care deeply for the hows of the world. That does of course not mean I can’t help you getting your whats & whys sharper, more realistic, or viable.

Highly motivated by doing things right and by a strong dislike for wasting energy, both my own and that of good talented people, I am passionate to find ways of doing things better. The average engineer turnover in our industry is something to be collectively ashamed of and I strive to build cultures that do better. The cost of software should not be expressed merely in terms of development costs: the cost of maintaining and operating software in perpetuity exceeds the cost of development many times over. That mindset is important and you want it present in your culture if you are playing a long term game. Developing anything is an expensive business to be in. You need someone with a strong sense of both business and engineering to steer the ship over the turbulent execution sea. That is the place where I get excited.

I attack problems with the analytical view of a software architect, but do not limit my scope to your mere software alone. I want to join you as an entrepeneurial partner, help you shape a fruitful future.

Tech lead at Cleverbase

May 2017 - April 2018

Originally hired as a software engineer I encountered a large gap in the devops departement. I began by creating a CI/CD pipeline and dockerizing deployments. Together with a colleague we did set up an AWS and colocated hardware hybrid that met the high standards of Qualified Trust Service Provider certification.

Met with growing pains and too much deployment impredicatability that slipped through the cracks over time I reverse engineered the network structure and implemented a maintainable and highly useful map, outline of the network that saved a lot of time (during maintenance and extension), brought calmness in the infra departement again and allowed me to concentrate on other areas of improvement in the company.

I coached developers, was responsible for setting the architectural course and upheld and set coding standards and processes (ISO / ETSI). I represented the engineering / development and test divisions in the management team, held one on one meetings with my team and was responsible for the technical hiring. I developed an assignment to effectively evaluate the skills of candidates.

CTO at Pallas Athena Group

February 2015 - July 2016

As the CTO of the Pallas Athena Group (mostly known for AthenaStudies) upon joining the company I came to the conclusion that the existing tech was not viable: hard to maintain, riddled with undefined and undeterministic behaviour. I led a rewrite of the existing functionality based upon a more flexible structure than what had existed before. Together with my business partner I brought the company from a bedroom to an office, with 10 employees.

For PAG I developed software, set architectural course, was leading a team with a frontenders and backenders and configured the CI/CD pipeline. The current team at Pallas Athena Group continued their work upon solid structures I built and / or started.

Projects as a contractor

April 2013 - March 2015, September 2016 - April 2017

This is a limited overview with only the most relevant projects mentioned.

1. Opiness

For Opiness I was asked to implemented an elastic search based search system. We created a system that implemented fuzzy searching, had location and distance ranges and was able to handle mucht larger volumes than the original solution it replaced.

Along the way I saw a chance to improve a particulary slow procedure for calculating review averages on large amounts of data. I reimplemented the weighted averaging formula in SQL, removing the extracting need and improving the speed from 24 hours to completion, to a mere 2 minutes. A key example of what I bring to the table: going an extra mile and looking creatively for ways to improve.

2. Insights ABN AMRO

For ABN AMRO I made a small system that aggregated the content of different blogs into a shared search system. Amongst these blogs a global search frontend allowed seemless switching between content from one site to another.

3. Pointers (powered by KPN)

Pointers was one of the most interesting and fun projects I have had the opportunity to work on.

It began when I was asked to consult on heavy scaling issues as the backend of the project could not handle the pressure of its success after becoming app of the day in the Apple store. It quickly became apparent that the backend was architected in a ways that were fundamentally flawed. After reaching this conclusion we reimplemented the backend with a team of the original designers (that knew al about what they wanted and provide ample guidance on what and why), a backend developer and an iOS developer. I was primairly involved in (storage) architecture, reimplementation / rethinking of the recomendation algorithms and helped develop the new software.

This project was a very clear example that you are not done when it seems to work and to know that and guide what follows is an important expertise.
